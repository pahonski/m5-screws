2. Identifying the Correct Fender Screws

Determining the precise screws for your ACID Integrated Carrier 3.0 involves understanding general standards, CUBE-specific details, and using available resources.

A. General Bicycle Fender Screw Info

Many bikes use M5 screws (M5x0.8mm thread) for fenders, often 8mm or 10mm long. Stainless steel is recommended for durability.

B. CUBE-Specifics: M4 vs. M5?

Some CUBE models might use M4 screws for certain mudguard fittings. This is a critical difference!

  • Test for M5: Try threading a standard bottle cage bolt (usually M5) into the fender mounting hole. If it fits smoothly, it's M5. If not, it could be M4.
  • Caution: Forcing an M5 screw into an M4 hole will damage threads. M4 screws are smaller and easier to cross-thread or strip if over-tightened.

4. Essential Tips for Fender Screw Installation

Correct installation is crucial for the longevity and proper function of your fender system.

🔩

Verify Screw Specifications

Double-check new screws against old ones or identified specs (diameter, pitch, length, head type).

🔧

Use the Correct Tools

Employ the right size and type of Allen key, Torx driver, or screwdriver to avoid damaging screw heads.

🖐️

Start Screws by Hand

Thread screws by hand for the first few turns to prevent cross-threading. If there's resistance, stop and realign.

⚖️

Proper Tightening Torque

Do not overtighten. Fender screws don't need high torque. For M4 screws, be especially gentle (around 4 Nm as a rough guide). Tighten until snug.

⚙️

Consider Washers and Thread Locker

Washers: Use if original hardware had them, to distribute force.
Thread Locker: Low-strength (blue) can prevent screws from vibrating loose.

🎬

Reference CUBE's Installation Video

Refer to the official video for assembly sequence and specific nuances of the ACID IC 3.0 system.

Why this matters: Correctly installed screws prevent rattles, fender rubbing, and damage to your bike's frame or carrier system. It ensures safety and a better riding experience.
